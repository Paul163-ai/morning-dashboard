## Verse

"The heavens declare the glory of God, and the sky above proclaims his
handiwork." — Psalm 19:1

## Explanation

Theologians distinguish two kinds of revelation. General revelation is
what God has made known through creation, conscience, and the ordinary
experience of being human — available, in principle, to everyone,
everywhere, without need of a Bible in hand. Special revelation is what
we looked at all last week: God's specific, verbal self-disclosure in
Scripture, and supremely in Christ. Today and tomorrow, we look at the
first of these.

David says the heavens themselves preach — silently, constantly,
everywhere, in every language, "their voice goes out through all the
earth" (v.4). A sunset, the vastness of space, the intricate design of a
living cell: none of it is neutral or silent about its Maker. Creation is
God's own handiwork, and it testifies to his existence, power, and glory
to everyone who has ever looked up at a sky or considered the natural
world, whether or not they've ever heard the gospel preached.

This matters because it means no one stands before God with a genuine
excuse of total ignorance. Creation itself has been speaking to every
person, everywhere, all along.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that creation itself testifies to your glory, everywhere
and always. Open my eyes today to notice what the heavens and the earth
are declaring about you, and let it move me to worship. Amen.

## Reflection Question

When did you last really notice creation "declaring the glory of God"
rather than just passing it by? What did you see?
