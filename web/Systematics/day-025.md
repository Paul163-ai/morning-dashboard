## Verse

"These are written so that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the
Son of God, and that by believing you may have life in his name." —
John 20:31

## Explanation

We come, in this final week of January, to sufficiency — the teaching that
Scripture contains everything necessary for salvation and for a godly
life, such that nothing needs to be added to it as a further requirement
for either. John's stated purpose for his own Gospel captures this
exactly: what he wrote was sufficient, on its own, to bring a reader to
saving faith in Christ. Not a starting point requiring supplementary
revelation to actually complete the job — sufficient, as given.

This has been an important, and at times contested, conviction throughout
church history. Various movements have claimed additional necessary
revelation — new prophecies, hidden traditions, further scriptures — as
required alongside the Bible for genuine salvation or spiritual maturity.
Sufficiency stands against all of this: everything you need to be saved,
you already have, in what God has already given.

This should be a settling, not a limiting, truth. You don't need a
mystical experience, a secret teaching, or an authority beyond Scripture
to know you can be saved. Whatever your background, your doubts, your
sense of spiritual poverty — the sufficient message is right there,
already given, already enough.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that I don't need anything beyond what you've already
given me in your word to be saved. Let that be enough for me, and keep me
from chasing after what you've already sufficiently provided. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Have you ever felt like you were missing some extra spiritual experience
or knowledge needed for real faith? How does Scripture's sufficiency
address that?
