## Verse

"Every word of God proves true; he is a shield to those who take refuge in
him." — Proverbs 30:5

## Explanation

Inerrancy is the claim that Scripture, in its original manuscripts, does
not affirm anything that is false — that everything it teaches, when
properly interpreted, is fully true, in matters of history, doctrine, and
morality alike. This isn't a claim about English translations being
flawless, or about every possible reading of a passage being correct; it's
a claim about the truthfulness of what the biblical authors, carried along
by the Spirit, actually intended to affirm.

It's worth being precise here, because inerrancy is often misunderstood
or caricatured. It doesn't mean Scripture uses only modern scientific
precision (it can describe a sunrise the way any observer would, without
that being a false statement about astronomy). It doesn't mean every
detail in parallel Gospel accounts must match word for word, the way a
courtroom transcript would (ancient biographical writing had different,
equally legitimate conventions than modern reporting). What it means is
this: Scripture, rightly understood, never actually deceives or misleads
you about what it is genuinely claiming to be true.

This grounds real confidence. If Scripture is God's own word, and God does
not lie (Titus 1:2), then Scripture's trustworthiness follows necessarily
from his own character. That connection — inerrancy flowing from who God
is — is where we turn tomorrow.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that every word you have given proves true. Give me
confidence in your word, not because I've resolved every difficulty, but
because I trust the character of the one who gave it. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Has a misunderstanding of what inerrancy actually claims ever caused you
unnecessary doubt about Scripture's trustworthiness?
