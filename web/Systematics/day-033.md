## Verse

"This is eternal life, that they know you the only true God, and Jesus
Christ whom you have sent." — John 17:3

## Explanation

Yesterday's truth needs today's balance, or it becomes a discouragement
rather than an invitation. Yes, God is incomprehensible — never fully
grasped by any finite mind. But Jesus says eternal life itself consists in
actually knowing God, personally and really, not merely knowing
true facts about him from a safe, permanent distance. Incomprehensibility
means you'll never know God exhaustively. It doesn't mean you can't know
him truly.

Think of it this way: you can know another person genuinely — their
character, their love, their faithfulness — without knowing every single
thought they've ever had or every experience that shaped them.
Comprehensive knowledge and true knowledge aren't the same thing. This is
even more true of God: what he has revealed about himself in Scripture is
completely reliable and sufficient for real relationship, even though it
doesn't exhaust who he is.

This should shape how you approach a whole year of theology. The goal was
never to master God intellectually, as though enough study could pin him
down like a specimen. The goal, as Jesus describes it, is life — real,
personal, relational knowledge of the God who has made himself known.
Hold incomprehensibility and knowability together: humble about the size
of the mystery, confident in the reality of the relationship.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that I can truly know you, even though I'll never
exhaust who you are. Let this year of theology deepen relationship with
you, not just accumulate facts about you. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is your pursuit of God this year aimed more at gathering information, or
at genuinely knowing him? What would help it be both?
