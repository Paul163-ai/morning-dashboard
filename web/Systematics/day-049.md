## Verse

"Great is our Lord, and abundant in power; his understanding is beyond
measure." — Psalm 147:5

## Explanation

Omniscience means God knows all things — every fact, every possibility,
every thought, every event across all of history and all of the future,
completely and simultaneously, without needing to learn, discover, or be
reminded of anything. His understanding, the Psalmist says, is "beyond
measure" — not merely vast, the way an encyclopedia is vast, but literally
without limit, encompassing everything there is or could be to know.

This includes things no human mind could ever access: your unspoken
thoughts (Psalm 139:2), events still entirely in the future (Isaiah
46:9–10), and even things that will never actually happen but could have
(1 Samuel 23:11–13, where God tells David what Saul's men would do in a
hypothetical scenario that never occurs). Nothing surprises God. No new
information ever reaches him that he didn't already, eternally, know.

This should shape trust more than anxiety. A God who knows everything —
including every detail of your circumstances, every hidden fear, every
future you can't yet see — is never caught off guard by what happens to
you, and never needs to be updated on your situation before he can help.
He already knows, completely, before you've even finished forming the
prayer.

## Prayer

Lord, your understanding is beyond measure — nothing about my life or my
future surprises you. Thank you that you already know everything I'm
facing, even before I've finished praying about it. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is there a situation you've been anxiously trying to explain fully to
God in prayer, as though he needed the details filled in? How does his
omniscience change that?
