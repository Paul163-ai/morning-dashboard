## Verse

"Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path." — Psalm 119:105

## Explanation

We close this week where inerrancy should ultimately lead: not to
defensive argument, but to confident, whole-hearted use of the entire
Bible. It's common, even among Christians, to quietly treat some parts of
Scripture as more trustworthy or relevant than others — the Gospels
prized, the Psalms cherished, but Leviticus, genealogies, or the harder
Old Testament narratives quietly skipped or treated as background noise.
If inerrancy is true, however, that instinct needs correcting. Every part
of Scripture, not just the comfortable or obviously devotional sections,
is genuinely a lamp for your feet.

This doesn't mean every passage carries identical weight for daily
application — a genealogy functions differently than a Gospel narrative,
and Scripture itself teaches you to read different genres differently.
But it does mean no part of the Bible can be safely ignored as
irrelevant or untrustworthy. The parts you find hardest to read are often
exactly the parts that reveal something about God's character, his
faithfulness across generations, or his holiness that the "easier"
passages don't show as clearly.

As you continue through this year, resist the temptation to only trust
and treasure the passages that already feel comfortable. Let the whole
counsel of Scripture, difficult parts included, actually be your light.

## Prayer

Father, thank you for the whole of your word, not just the comfortable
parts. Give me appetite for the passages I've tended to skip, and let all
of Scripture, not just my favourites, genuinely light my path. Amen.

## Reflection Question

What part of the Bible have you tended to skip or treat as less
important? What might you be missing?
