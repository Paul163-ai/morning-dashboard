## Verse

"Repent therefore, and turn again, that your sins may be blotted out." —
Acts 3:19

## Explanation

We move now to conversion — the human side of coming to Christ, made up of
two inseparable responses: repentance and faith. Today, repentance.

The word Peter uses pictures a change of mind that leads to a change of
direction — not just feeling bad about sin, but actually turning away from
it, toward God. It's the same idea behind the prodigal son "coming to
himself" and turning for home (Luke 15:17–20): a real reversal, not just
regret felt while continuing on the same path.

Repentance isn't a single emotional moment reserved for the day you first
believed. It's the posture of continually turning — recognising sin for
what it is, agreeing with God about it rather than excusing it, and
actually walking away from it rather than merely apologising and
continuing. This is why repentance and faith are always paired in
Scripture (Mark 1:15) — you can't trust Christ as Saviour while still
clinging to what he came to save you from. Turning to him necessarily means
turning from something else.

If there's an area of sin you've confessed many times but never actually
turned from, today's a good day to ask whether what's happened has been
repentance or just regret. God's promise attached to real repentance is
striking: sins blotted out, wiped clean, gone.

## Prayer

Lord, search me for the difference between regret and real repentance.
Where I've confessed sin but kept walking toward it, give me grace to
actually turn. Thank you that turned-from sin is sin you promise to blot
out completely. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is there a sin you've regretted repeatedly but never truly turned away
from? What would turning look like, practically, this week?
