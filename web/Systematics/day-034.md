## Verse

"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the
name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit." —
Matthew 28:19

## Explanation

We arrive now at the doctrine that sits at the very centre of Christian
belief about who God is: the Trinity. Notice the careful wording of
Jesus' own command — baptizing in the singular "name" (not "names") of
the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Three persons, named individually, and
yet one name, one God. This single verse contains the whole shape of the
doctrine in miniature: real distinction between Father, Son, and Spirit,
held together in undivided unity.

The word "Trinity" never appears in Scripture, but the reality it
describes runs through the entire New Testament — three persons,
consistently addressed and treated as fully divine, and yet Scripture
never wavers from its insistence that there is only one God (Deuteronomy
6:4, 1 Corinthians 8:6). This isn't a contradiction the church invented a
clever formula to paper over. It's the actual, given shape of how God has
revealed himself: genuinely three, genuinely one, in ways that exceed
full human comprehension while still being clearly, repeatedly taught.

This week we'll take this slowly, a piece at a time — this most central,
most mysterious, and most important truth about who God actually is.

## Prayer

Triune God, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — I don't fully understand you,
but I worship you as you've revealed yourself: three persons, one God.
Teach me this week to know you more truly. Amen.
