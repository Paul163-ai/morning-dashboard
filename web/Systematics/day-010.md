## Verse

"Then the Lord put out his hand and touched my mouth. And the Lord said to
me, 'Behold, I have put my words in your mouth.'" — Jeremiah 1:9

## Explanation

Open almost any Old Testament prophetic book and you'll find some version
of the same phrase repeated constantly: "thus says the Lord." The
prophets themselves didn't claim to be offering wise religious opinions
or spiritual insights they'd arrived at through reflection. They claimed,
consistently and specifically, to be relaying words God had directly given
them — as Jeremiah describes here, words placed in his mouth by God's own
hand.

This is worth taking seriously precisely because it's such an enormous
claim. If it weren't true, the honest response would be to dismiss the
Old Testament prophets as either deceivers or deluded. But the claim
itself is the consistent, self-understanding of the text: not "here is
what I think about God," but "here is what God himself has said." The
prophets' own confidence in the source of their words is part of why the
church has always received the Old Testament as Scripture in the fullest
sense — God's own words, given through chosen human mouths.

This should shape how seriously we take the Old Testament, not as
background material to the "real" revelation in the New Testament, but as
authoritative divine speech in its own right, carrying the same weight as
anything Christ or the apostles later said.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you for the prophets who faithfully carried your very words.
Help me receive the Old Testament with the same seriousness and authority
I give the New. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Do you tend to treat the Old Testament as less authoritative or less
relevant than the New? How does today's verse challenge that?
