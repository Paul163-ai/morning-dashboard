## Verse

"Where shall I go from your Spirit? Or where shall I flee from your
presence? If I ascend to heaven, you are there! If I make my bed in
Sheol, you are there!" — Psalm 139:7–8

## Explanation

Omnipresence is the teaching that God is fully present everywhere at
once — not spread thin across the universe like a substance divided into
parts, and not present in some places more than others, but wholly
present in every location simultaneously, in a way no creature could ever
be. David's poetry in Psalm 139 explores this from every angle: heaven,
the depths, the farthest sea — everywhere he could imagine going, God is
already there, fully, not partially.

This truth cuts two ways, and David feels both. On one hand, it's
inescapable in a way that could feel unsettling — there truly is nowhere
to hide from God, no private corner of your life he isn't fully present
to witness. Every thought, every action, every hidden struggle happens in
his immediate presence, not observed from a distance but genuinely,
fully present to.

On the other hand — and this is where David's psalm actually lands, as
we'll see tomorrow — the very truth that could terrify becomes, for
someone who knows and trusts God, immense comfort. There is no place so
dark, so far, or so isolated that God's presence hasn't already reached
there ahead of you.

## Prayer

Lord, you are present everywhere — nothing about me is hidden from you.
Help me live today aware of your presence in every moment, not evading it
but resting in it. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Does the truth that "there is nowhere to hide from God" currently feel
more unsettling or more comforting to you? Why?
