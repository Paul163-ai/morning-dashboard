## Verse

"The times of ignorance God overlooked, but now he commands all people
everywhere to repent." — Acts 17:30

## Explanation

One thing worth stating plainly: the gospel call is not a trick or a
formality offered to people God secretly intends to reject. Paul, standing
in Athens before people who had no covenant history with God at all,
doesn't say "some of you might be interested" — he says God commands all
people everywhere to repent. The invitation is as wide as the human race.

This matters because it's possible to hold the doctrine of election in a
way that quietly undermines the sincerity of the gospel offer, as if the
"whoever" in Scripture secretly means "whoever happens to be elect." But
Scripture doesn't talk that way. It presents the call as a real, urgent,
loving command to every single person, and treats refusal as genuine
culpable unbelief, not a foregone technicality (John 3:18–19). God is not
playing games with anyone who hears the gospel and turns away.

So hold both truths without letting one cancel the other: God's sovereign
election stands behind salvation, and God's genuine, wholehearted
invitation stands in front of it, addressed to you and to everyone who
hears. When you share the gospel with someone, you're not offering a
possibility hedged with fine print. You're relaying a real command and a
real invitation from God himself.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that your call to repent is sincere and wide open — not
just to some, but to all people everywhere, including me. Help me offer
Christ to others with the same wholehearted sincerity you show. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Have you ever softened or hesitated to share the gospel fully because you
weren't sure if the person was "one of the elect"? How does today's verse
address that hesitation?
