## Verse

"Oh, taste and see that the Lord is good! Blessed is the man who takes
refuge in him!" — Psalm 34:8

## Explanation

God's goodness means he is the source and the very standard of
everything that is truly good — not good because he happens to conform
to some external moral standard that exists independently of him, but
good in such a way that his own character defines what goodness actually
is. This is an important distinction: goodness isn't a rule God obeys
from outside himself; it's who he is, and everything genuinely good in
creation reflects, in some measure, an attribute that belongs first and
supremely to him.

David's invitation — "taste and see" — is deliberately experiential
language, not just intellectual assertion. Goodness isn't only something
to affirm as a doctrine; it's something meant to be personally
experienced, discovered, savoured in the actual business of taking refuge
in God through real circumstances, the way you'd discover a food is good
by actually tasting it rather than reading a description.

This matters because it settles, in advance, the question of whether
God's actions and commands are actually good, even when they're hard to
understand or accept. Whatever God does flows from his own perfectly
good character. The standard for measuring whether something is good was
never going to be found somewhere outside of him — it's found in who he
already, unchangeably, is.

## Prayer

Lord, you are good — not measured against some outside standard, but the
very definition of goodness itself. Let me taste and see your goodness
today in the actual details of my life, not just affirm it as an idea.
Amen.

## Reflection Question

Where in your life recently have you actually "tasted" God's goodness,
rather than just believed in it abstractly?
