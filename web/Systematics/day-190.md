## Verse

"I endure everything for the sake of the elect, that they also may obtain
the salvation that is in Christ Jesus with eternal glory." — 2 Timothy 2:10

## Explanation

If you've ever wondered whether believing in election should make someone
passive about evangelism — why bother if God has already decided — Paul's
own life answers that directly. He calls himself a prisoner, endures
hardship, and pours out his energy in ministry, and the reason he gives is
"for the sake of the elect." Election, for Paul, wasn't a reason to stop
proclaiming Christ. It was his fuel for doing it relentlessly.

Here's why that isn't a contradiction: God doesn't just ordain the end
(that certain people will be saved) — he ordains the means (that they'll be
saved through hearing and believing the gospel, proclaimed by people like
Paul, and like you). Election doesn't make evangelism unnecessary; it makes
it certain to succeed. When you share Christ, you're not trying to
persuade God's hand or hoping to tip an uncertain outcome. You're taking
part in exactly the means God has always intended to use to gather his
chosen people to himself.

This is where a full week thinking about election should land: not in
speculation about who is or isn't chosen, but in the same confidence and
energy Paul had. You don't know who the elect are among the people around
you. But you know the gospel is the means God uses. That should make you
bolder, not quieter.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that my witness isn't wasted effort, but the very means you
use to call your people to yourself. Give me Paul's endurance and boldness
to keep speaking of Christ, trusting you with the outcome. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Who in your life could you speak to about Christ this week, trusting God
with the results rather than waiting for certainty first?
