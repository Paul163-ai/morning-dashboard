## Verse

"He chose us in him before the foundation of the world... he predestined us
for adoption to himself as sons through Jesus Christ, according to the
purpose of his will." — Ephesians 1:4–5

## Explanation

This is one of the most staggering claims in Scripture, and it's easy to
rush past it. Before there was a world, before there was a you, God had
already set his love on you and determined to bring you to himself through
Christ. Your salvation didn't begin the day you believed — it began in the
counsels of God before time existed.

Election is the doctrine that God, from eternity, chose those who would be
saved — not because of anything he foresaw in them, but "according to the
purpose of his will." That phrase is important. It rules out the idea that
God looked ahead, saw who would choose him, and picked accordingly. The
choosing is his, first and entirely, rooted in nothing outside his own will
and love.

This doctrine unsettles some people and comforts others, often depending on
what they think it implies about God's character. Over the next few days
we'll work through the hard questions it raises. But start here, with what
Paul actually emphasises: election is "in Christ," and its purpose is
adoption — becoming beloved children in a family. Whatever else is true
about election, its aim was never cold calculation. It was love, set on you,
before you existed to earn or deserve it.

## Prayer

Father, it's almost too much to take in — that you set your love on me
before the world began. Thank you that my standing with you doesn't rest on
anything I've done, but on your purpose and will. Help me receive this as
the love it is. Amen.

## Reflection Question

How does knowing God's choice of you predates anything you've ever done
change the way you think about your own worth before him?
