## Verse

"The steadfast love of the Lord never ceases; his mercies never come to
an end; they are new every morning; great is your faithfulness." —
Lamentations 3:22–23

## Explanation

On a day the world associates with love, it's worth connecting yesterday's
doctrine directly to this one: God's eternity is precisely what makes his
love inexhaustible. A love bound by time, like human love, is finite —
it can tire, fade with distance, or simply run its course over enough
years. God's steadfast love, Jeremiah says even in the middle of writing
Lamentations, a book soaked in grief, never ceases. It has no closing
chapter, because the God whose character it flows from has no beginning
or end to run out.

Notice where this confidence appears: not in a season of ease, but in
the aftermath of Jerusalem's devastating fall, amid genuine national
catastrophe and personal grief. If God's eternal, unceasing love could
be trusted there, in the middle of ruins, it can be trusted in whatever
your own circumstances hold today. His mercies being "new every morning"
means this isn't a love you're slowly using up through your failures or
your need — it renews, completely, every single day, precisely because
its source is eternal, not a finite reservoir.

Whatever human love has disappointed or limited you, God's love, rooted
in his own eternal, unchanging nature, has no such limit.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you for a love that never ceases and mercies that are new
every morning. Where human love has disappointed or run dry, let your
eternal, unlimited love be enough. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Where in your life has human love felt limited or run dry? How does
God's inexhaustible love speak into that specific place?
