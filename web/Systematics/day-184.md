## Verse

"He did not leave himself without witness, for he did good by giving you
rains from heaven and fruitful seasons, satisfying your hearts with food and
gladness." — Acts 14:17

## Explanation

Paul said this to a crowd of pagans in Lystra who had no knowledge of
Scripture at all. His point wasn't "you already know the gospel" — it was
"you already have evidence that God is good, generous, and present." Every
harvest, every meal, every good season is a small sermon preached by a God
who has not left the world to fend for itself.

Common grace doesn't just work through weather and crops. It restrains
human evil through government, law, and conscience (Romans 13:1–4), so that
society doesn't collapse into total chaos. It gifts unbelievers with wisdom,
skill, and creativity, so that good governments can function, good medicine
can be practiced, and good art can be made — often by people who give God no
credit at all. None of this is salvation. But it is God actively upholding a
world that has rejected him, for reasons of his own mercy and patience.

This should shape how a Christian engages with culture. You don't need to
treat every institution, invention, or insight outside the church as
worthless or hostile. Much of it is common grace — gifts God has scattered
generously, meant to be received with thanks even by those who don't yet
know the Giver.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you for the ordinary gifts of a functioning world — food,
government, medicine, art, wisdom — even where they come through people who
don't yet honour you. Teach me to receive your common grace with gratitude,
and to point past the gift to the Giver. Amen.

## Reflection Question

What "ordinary" gift in your life today might actually be evidence of God's
patient, generous common grace?
