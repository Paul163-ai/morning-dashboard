## Verse

"Long ago, at many times and in many ways, God spoke to our fathers by the
prophets, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son." —
Hebrews 1:1–2

## Explanation

If general revelation is God's testimony through creation, special
revelation is God's direct, specific self-disclosure — through the
prophets, through Scripture, and supremely, the author of Hebrews says,
through his own Son. This is revelation of an entirely different kind:
not the silent preaching of a sunset, but God actually speaking, in
words, at particular times, to particular people, about particular
things — chiefly, how sinners can be reconciled to him.

Notice the progression Hebrews describes: God spoke "at many times and in
many ways" through the prophets across the Old Testament, building toward
a single, climactic, final Word — his Son. Everything special revelation
has ever done was heading toward Christ, and everything Scripture records
either anticipates him or explains him. This is why Christians read the
whole Bible, Old and New Testament together, as a unified account of
God's special revelation, not two separate religions loosely stitched
together.

Special revelation is where you find what general revelation could never
supply: the actual content of the gospel, the person and work of Christ,
and the specific promises God has made to those who trust him.

## Prayer

Father, thank you for speaking specifically, not just generally — for the
prophets, the Scriptures, and above all for your Son, the fullest word you
have ever spoken. Let me treasure what general revelation could never
give me. Amen.
