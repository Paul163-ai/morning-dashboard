## Verse

"The law of the Lord is perfect, reviving the soul... the rules of the
Lord are true, and righteous altogether. More to be desired are they than
gold." — Psalm 19:7, 9–10

## Explanation

We close this whole first month, and the doctrine of Scripture itself,
with contentment. David doesn't describe God's word as adequate,
sufficient in some minimal, bare sense — he describes it as perfect, more
desirable than gold, sweeter than honey (v.10). Four weeks ago we started
with the claim that Scripture is God's own breathed-out word. We end here,
with the natural response to that truth: not restless searching for
something more, but deep satisfaction in what has actually been given.

This is worth pausing on before the year moves forward into new
territory tomorrow. Everything we've studied this month — Scripture's
necessity, its dual authorship, its authority over every rival, its
truthfulness, its clarity, its sufficiency — has been building toward this
posture: settled trust and genuine delight in God's word, rather than
restless dissatisfaction, doubt, or the constant hunt for something
Scripture supposedly lacks.

Tomorrow we begin an entirely new section — the doctrine of God himself,
starting with the most basic question of all: how do we even know he
exists? Everything we study from here forward will rest on the foundation
this month has laid: a book that is God's own trustworthy word, sufficient
for knowing him and living for him, worth treasuring far more than gold.

## Prayer

Father, thank you for a whole month spent in your word about your word.
Give me David's delight — not grudging acceptance, but genuine desire for
Scripture, more than gold, sweeter than honey. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Looking back over this month, what's one truth about Scripture that has
most deepened your trust or delight in it?
