## Verse

"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever
believes in him should not perish but have eternal life." — John 3:16

## Explanation

Yesterday's doctrine can sound, on first hearing, almost cold — a God who
needs nothing might seem distant or indifferent. The opposite is true, and
it's worth sitting with why. Because God's love for you doesn't spring
from any lack in himself, it can never be transactional, conditional, or
insecure in the way human love so often is. A person who needs your love
to feel complete will, consciously or not, hold you a little hostage to
that need. God's love is not like that. He gave his Son to save you not
because he needed you to complete him, but purely because he chose,
freely, to love.

This is what makes grace actually gracious. If God needed something from
you — your worship, your obedience, your love in return — to be happy or
whole, then his kindness toward you would ultimately be self-interested,
however dressed up. Because he needs nothing, everything he gives is
pure gift, offered from overflowing fullness rather than calculated need.
His love for you cost him everything, and gained him, in the sense of
completing or fulfilling him, nothing he didn't already eternally possess.

That's a love worth resting your whole life on: utterly free, utterly
secure, never contingent on what you can offer in return.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that your love for me was never about what you needed
from me. It was pure gift, freely given at great cost, from a God who
already lacked nothing. Let me rest in a love this secure. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Does knowing God's love isn't transactional change how safe or secure his
love feels to you?
