## Verse

"And if children, then heirs — heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ,
provided we suffer with him in order that we may also be glorified with
him." — Romans 8:17

## Explanation

We end July on the future adoption secures. In most families, an heir
receives a portion of what the parent owned. Paul's language here is more
extravagant: believers are heirs of God himself, and "fellow heirs with
Christ" — not receiving a portion set aside for adopted children while
Christ, the true Son, receives the rest, but sharing fully in the same
inheritance as the Son himself. That's the staggering scope of what
adoption ultimately secures: not just present belonging, but a promised,
shared future glory.

Paul doesn't leave out the qualifier, though — "provided we suffer with
him." Adoption into this family doesn't exempt you from hardship in this
life; if anything, it guarantees you'll walk the same road Christ walked,
suffering now, glory later. That's not a contradiction of the family
picture — it's part of it. Children in a family share not just the
inheritance but the story, including its hard chapters, on the way to its
ending.

We've now traced the whole arc of this month: common grace, election, the
gospel call, regeneration, conversion, justification, and adoption —
God's grace reaching down before you existed, calling you, remaking you,
declaring you righteous, and welcoming you as an heir of everything. Next
month picks up the next chapter: sanctification, the lifelong process of
actually becoming what you already, legally and relationally, are.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that I am not just forgiven but adopted, not just
adopted but an heir of everything that belongs to Christ. Whatever
suffering this life holds, let me walk it as your child, certain of the
glory still to come. Amen.
