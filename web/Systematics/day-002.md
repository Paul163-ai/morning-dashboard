## Verse

"For my thoughts are not your thoughts, neither are your ways my ways,
declares the Lord. For as the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my
ways higher than your ways and my thoughts than your thoughts." —
Isaiah 55:8–9

## Explanation

Human beings have always tried to reach God — or a god — through
speculation: philosophy, mysticism, moral striving, careful observation of
the world. Some of that instinct is good and God-given, and we'll look at
it tomorrow. But Isaiah is blunt about its limits. God's thoughts and ways
sit at a height human reasoning simply cannot climb to unaided. Left alone
with our best thinking, we would still be guessing at who God is —
inventing gods in our own image, as every culture in history has done to
some degree.

This is the deep reason revelation is necessary, not optional. It's not
that God is unusually shy or that theology is an unusually hard subject.
It's that the gap between infinite Creator and finite creature cannot be
closed from our side, however hard we try. If we are to know God truly,
rather than merely construct plausible ideas about him, he has to be the
one to close the gap — by speaking.

That's a humbling starting point for a whole year of theology. Everything
that follows depends not on how clever or careful our reasoning is, but on
whether we're actually listening to what God has said, rather than quietly
substituting our own preferred version of him.

## Prayer

Lord, I confess how easily I construct a version of you shaped by my own
preferences rather than listening to who you've revealed yourself to be.
Humble my reasoning under your revelation this year. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is there a belief about God you hold that you've never actually checked
against Scripture — one you've simply assumed or reasoned your way to?
