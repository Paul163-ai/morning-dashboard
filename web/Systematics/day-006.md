## Verse

"For his invisible attributes, namely, his eternal power and divine
nature, have been clearly perceived, ever since the creation of the world,
in the things that have been made. So they are without excuse." —
Romans 1:20

## Explanation

General revelation is genuinely revelation — Paul says God's power and
divine nature are "clearly perceived," not vaguely hinted at. But notice
what Paul draws from this: people are "without excuse," not "without
need of the gospel." General revelation is enough to hold every person
accountable for suppressing what they could plainly see about God (v.18).
It is not enough to save anyone.

This is an important distinction to hold carefully. General revelation
can tell you that God exists, that he is powerful, that he is glorious.
It cannot tell you that he sent his Son to die for sinners, that
salvation comes by grace through faith, or how to be reconciled to him.
That content — the gospel itself — comes only through special revelation:
Scripture, and the preaching of Christ crucified and risen. Nature has
never saved anyone; it has only ever left people accountable, and, left
to itself, it tends to be suppressed and distorted into idolatry rather
than followed toward the true God (Romans 1:21–23).

This is why missions and gospel preaching remain urgent, not optional
extras for people who happen to be interested. General revelation
condemns; only special revelation saves.

## Prayer

Father, thank you for the ways creation testifies to you — and thank you
even more for the specific, saving word of the gospel. Give me urgency to
share that word with those who have only ever had the first without the
second. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Who do you know who seems to have a vague awareness of God through
creation, but has never heard the specific gospel of Christ clearly? What
would it look like to tell them?
