## Verse

"So for the sake of your tradition you have made void the word of God." —
Matthew 15:6

## Explanation

Jesus said this to religious leaders who had built an elaborate system of
tradition around the law — not necessarily evil in itself, but who had
allowed that tradition to quietly override what God had actually
commanded. Their specific error was using a tradition about dedicating
money to God as a loophole to avoid caring for aging parents, directly
contradicting the fifth commandment. Tradition had become, functionally,
a higher authority than Scripture itself.

Tradition isn't automatically the enemy — every church has practices,
habits, and inherited wisdom that can be genuinely good, tested and
refined over generations. But tradition must always remain answerable to
Scripture, never the reverse. When a church's long-standing practice, a
denomination's historic stance, or a family's inherited religious habits
conflict with what Scripture actually teaches, Scripture wins — not
because tradition has no value, but because only Scripture carries the
authority of God's own word.

This is worth examining honestly. Every Christian tradition, including
whichever one shaped you, carries some inherited assumptions never fully
tested against Scripture itself. Submitting to Scripture's authority means
being willing to ask, even of cherished tradition: is this actually what
God has said, or is this simply what we've always done?

## Prayer

Lord, show me where tradition — even good, familiar tradition — has
quietly taken a place above your actual word in my life or church. Give me
courage to submit tradition to Scripture, not the other way around. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is there a practice or belief you've inherited from your church or
family tradition that you've never actually tested against what Scripture
says?
