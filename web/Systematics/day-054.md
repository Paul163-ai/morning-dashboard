## Verse

"Know therefore that the Lord your God is God, the faithful God who keeps
covenant and steadfast love with those who love him and keep his
commandments, to a thousand generations." — Deuteronomy 7:9

## Explanation

Truthfulness in what God says leads naturally to faithfulness in what he
does — keeping every promise, honouring every covenant, following through
on every commitment across however much time it takes. Moses tells
Israel this isn't a general character trait tested only occasionally, but
a settled fact spanning "a thousand generations" — essentially, forever,
by ancient reckoning. God's faithfulness doesn't have a shelf life or an
expiry date past which his promises become uncertain.

This matters especially for promises that seem slow to be fulfilled.
Israel waited centuries between promise and fulfilment more than once —
Abraham was promised descendants and land long before either fully
materialised; the whole Old Testament waits, across generations, for the
Messiah eventually to come. God's faithfulness was never in question
during those long waits; only the timing, held entirely in his own wise
hands, remained unclear to those living through them.

If you're currently waiting on a promise from God that feels
long-delayed, this is worth remembering: delay is not the same as
unfaithfulness. The God who kept covenant across a thousand generations
with Israel has not become any less faithful in the time you've spent
waiting on him.

## Prayer

Faithful God, thank you for keeping covenant across generations, even
when fulfilment felt slow. Where I'm waiting on you now, help me trust
your faithfulness rather than doubt it because of the delay. Amen.

## Reflection Question

What promise or hope are you currently waiting on God for? How does his
track record of faithfulness across long waits encourage you?
