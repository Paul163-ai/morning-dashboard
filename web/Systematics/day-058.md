## Verse

"Anyone who does not love does not know God, because God is love." —
1 John 4:8

## Explanation

We end February moving into an attribute that will carry us into March:
love. John's statement is easy to read too quickly. He doesn't say God
is loving, as one quality among his many other qualities — he says God
is love, identifying love with God's very essence, as fundamental to who
he is as anything else true about him. This means human love, at its
very best, isn't the standard God's love gets measured against. It's the
other way entirely: God's love is the original, and every genuine human
experience of love is, at best, a faint, borrowed reflection of
something that belongs first and supremely to him.

This matters because it's tempting to build your idea of God's love out
of the best human love you've experienced or imagined — a devoted
parent, a loyal friend, a romantic ideal. Those are real reflections, but
they remain reflections, always partial, sometimes distorted by human
limitation and sin. God's love isn't scaled-up human love. It's the
source human love has always been dimly imitating, and the only love
that can never disappoint the way human love sometimes inevitably does.

This week, and into next, we'll trace what this love actually looks
like — not sentiment, but active, self-giving commitment, most fully
revealed at the cross.

## Prayer

Father, you are love — not merely loving, but love itself, the source
every human love only dimly reflects. Teach me this week to know your
love as it truly is, not just as I've imagined it from human examples.
Amen.

## Reflection Question

Has your picture of God's love been shaped more by Scripture, or by the
best (or worst) human love you've personally experienced?
