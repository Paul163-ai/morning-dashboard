## Verse

"For I the Lord do not change; therefore you, O children of Jacob, are
not consumed." — Malachi 3:6

## Explanation

Unchangeableness — sometimes called immutability — is the teaching that
God's character, purposes, and promises remain constant, never shifting,
growing, diminishing, or being revised. This doesn't mean God is static
or inactive; he acts, responds to prayer, and relates dynamically to his
creation throughout Scripture. What it means is that his essential
nature — his holiness, love, justice, wisdom, and faithfulness — never
changes, and his eternal purposes never need correcting or adjusting
because they were flawed or mistaken to begin with.

Malachi draws out a striking implication: Israel's continued existence,
despite generations of unfaithfulness, rests directly on God's
unchanging character. If God's covenant love could shift with his mood
or their performance, Israel would have been consumed long ago. Their
survival is direct evidence of a God who does not change his mind about
his own commitments.

This matters enormously for anyone who has ever wondered whether God's
attitude toward them shifts with their own inconsistency — better on good
days, cooler on bad ones. His unchanging nature means the answer is no.
The same God who loved you at your conversion loves you exactly the same,
with the same character and commitment, today.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that you do not change — not your character, not your
promises, not your commitment to your people. When my own consistency
wavers, let your unchanging nature be my anchor. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Have you ever felt like God's attitude toward you shifts based on your
own performance? How does his unchangeableness address that?
