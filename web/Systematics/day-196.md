## Verse

"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has
passed away; behold, the new has come." — 2 Corinthians 5:17

## Explanation

We close this week on regeneration with the question that naturally
follows: how do you know if it has happened to you? Not by feeling for an
invisible inner event, but by looking for visible fruit. Paul doesn't say
"you might feel different" — he says something has actually changed: the
old has passed away, the new has come. Regeneration always produces real,
traceable effects in a life.

John describes some of these marks elsewhere in his letters: a settled
practice of righteousness rather than habitual sin (1 John 3:9), genuine
love for other believers (1 John 3:14), and belief that Jesus is the
Christ (1 John 5:1). None of these mean sinless perfection — every
Christian still fights sin daily, as we'll see when we get to
sanctification. But they mean direction has changed. A regenerate person
is someone now moving toward Christlikeness, however imperfectly, rather
than someone entirely indifferent or hostile to it.

If you're asking whether you've been born again, don't search for a
feeling. Ask instead: has your desire changed? Do you love Christ, want to
please him, and grieve when you fail him in ways you didn't before? That
ongoing movement, however small, is the mark of the new creation Paul
describes — evidence of a work only God could have done.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you for the real, visible change your Spirit has worked in me.
Where I still fall short, keep me moving forward rather than standing
still. Let the fruit of new life in me grow more evident over time. Amen.

## Reflection Question

What's one specific way your desires or direction have genuinely changed
since coming to know Christ?
