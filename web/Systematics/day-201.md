## Verse

"For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own
doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may
boast." — Ephesians 2:8–9

## Explanation

Even faith itself, Paul says, is not something you produced from your own
resources to earn God's favour — it's given. This might sound like a small
distinction, but it protects something essential: if faith were simply a
achievement you generated, salvation would still, ultimately, rest on
something you did. Paul closes that door entirely. Grace saves; faith is
the empty hand that receives it, and even that empty hand is enabled by
God, "so that no one may boast."

This is why faith is so often pictured in Scripture not as effort but as
reception — receiving Christ (John 1:12), receiving the free gift of
righteousness (Romans 5:17), receiving the kingdom like a child (Mark
10:15). A gift, by definition, isn't earned by the one receiving it; it's
simply taken hold of. Faith doesn't add anything to Christ's finished
work. It's the hand that takes what he has already accomplished.

If you've ever felt like your faith wasn't "strong enough" to save you,
remember what's actually doing the saving: not the strength of your grip,
but the sufficiency of what you're gripping. A weak hand still receives
the gift, as long as it's genuinely reaching for Christ and not itself.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that even my faith is your gift, not my achievement. I
have nothing to boast in — only Christ, who I receive by grace alone.
Strengthen my grip, but let me trust in what I'm holding, not the strength
of my hold. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Have you ever worried your faith wasn't "strong enough"? How does knowing
faith is a gift, not an achievement, change that worry?
