## Verse

"Yet for us there is one God, the Father, from whom are all things and for
whom we exist." — 1 Corinthians 8:6

## Explanation

Over the next three days, we look individually at each person of the
Trinity, beginning with the one most readily called "God" in ordinary
speech: the Father. Paul's confession here is thoroughly monotheistic and
Jewish in shape — one God, the Father, source and goal of all things —
while, in the very next breath, adding "and one Lord, Jesus Christ,
through whom are all things," placing Christ within that same single
divine identity Paul has just confessed. We'll get to why that matters
for the Son tomorrow.

For today, focus simply on the Father as fully, truly God: the one who
planned salvation before the world began (Ephesians 1:3–5), who sent the
Son (John 3:16), who is addressed throughout Scripture as Creator,
Sustainer, and covenant God of Israel. This might seem like the least
controversial claim of the week — of course the Father is God. But it's
worth stating plainly precisely because it grounds everything else: the
Trinity isn't the Father plus two lesser additions. It's Father, Son, and
Spirit, each equally, fully, the one true God, in eternal relationship
with one another.

## Prayer

Father, thank you for being the source of all things, the one who
planned my salvation before I existed and sent your Son to accomplish it.
I worship you as fully God, first person of the eternal Trinity. Amen.

## Reflection Question

How does knowing the Father planned your salvation before the world
began shape how you think about his care for you specifically?
