## Verse

"God is not man, that he should lie, or a son of man, that he should
change his mind." — Numbers 23:19

## Explanation

Truthfulness means that everything God says is true, and everything he
is corresponds perfectly to how he presents himself — no deception, no
exaggeration, no gap between appearance and reality. This isn't simply
that God chooses not to lie, as though it were a temptation he
successfully resists. Scripture goes further: Titus 1:2 calls him "God,
who never lies," describing lying as something outside the very
possibility of his nature, not merely outside his practice.

This grounds something we touched on back in January when studying
inerrancy: Scripture can be fully trusted precisely because the God who
breathed it out cannot lie. If truthfulness weren't part of God's
unchanging character, nothing else in the Bible could be relied upon
with real confidence — every promise, every doctrine, every comfort
offered in Scripture ultimately rests on this foundation: the one who
said it cannot lie.

This should reshape how you hold every promise you find in Scripture.
Not as hopeful possibilities, or well-intentioned statements that might
not fully pan out, but as certainties, grounded in the very nature of the
God who cannot say what isn't true. When God promises something, the
question isn't whether he's being honest. That's settled. The only
remaining question is how and when he'll fulfil it.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that you cannot lie — that everything you have promised
me is certain, not merely hopeful. Let me hold your promises with the
confidence your truthful character deserves. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is there a promise from Scripture you've been holding loosely, more as a
hope than a certainty? What would it look like to hold it as settled,
given God's truthful nature?
