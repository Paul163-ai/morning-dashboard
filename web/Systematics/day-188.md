## Verse

"All that the Father gives me will come to me, and whoever comes to me I
will never cast out." — John 6:37

## Explanation

Notice how Jesus holds two things together in a single sentence without any
sense of contradiction. First: the Father gives people to the Son — a
statement of God's sovereign initiative, the same truth we've been tracing
in election. Second: whoever comes will be received — an open invitation to
anyone who chooses to come. Jesus doesn't feel the need to resolve the
tension between these; he simply states both as true.

This is the pattern all through Scripture. God is sovereign over who is
saved, and every person who is saved comes by their own genuine choice,
believing and repenting freely. Scripture never treats these as competing
explanations where you must pick one and discard the other. It treats them
as two true things, seen from two vantage points — from above, salvation is
entirely God's gracious initiative; from where you stand, it is your real
decision to trust Christ.

Practically, this means election is never a reason to hold back from
inviting anyone to Christ, as though only some are eligible to hear. The
call goes out to "whoever" — and the promise attached to it is wonderfully
unconditional: whoever comes will never be cast out. If you have come to
Christ, or are drawn to come today, that itself is invitation enough. You
don't need to first solve the mystery of election to respond to the offer.

## Prayer

Lord Jesus, thank you that your promise to "whoever comes" is wide open. I
don't need to understand everything about your sovereign purposes before I
come to you — I only need to come. Draw me to yourself today, and never
cast me out. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Does the mystery of election tend to make you hesitant to share Christ with
others, or free to offer him to anyone? Why?
