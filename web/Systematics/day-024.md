## Verse

"The natural person does not accept the things of the Spirit of God, for
they are folly to him, and he is not able to understand them because they
are spiritually discerned." — 1 Corinthians 2:14

## Explanation

We close this week with an important qualifier: Scripture's clarity is
real, but it's not automatic or mechanical, as if anyone reading the text
correctly must arrive at understanding regardless of their spiritual
state. Paul is clear that spiritual truth is spiritually discerned — a
person without the Spirit can read the very same words and find them
foolish, not because the text is unclear, but because true understanding
of spiritual reality requires the Spirit's own illuminating work in the
reader, not just accurate eyesight on the page.

This is why prayer belongs alongside Bible reading, not as a formality but
as a genuine request: asking God, by his Spirit, to open your eyes to
what you're reading (Psalm 119:18). It's also why reading in community —
alongside other believers, teachers, and the accumulated wisdom of the
church across history — remains valuable even given Scripture's clarity.
None of this contradicts what we've studied this week. It completes it:
Scripture is clear enough for ordinary believers to understand, and God
has designed that understanding to come through humble dependence on his
Spirit, in the company of his people, not through isolated human cleverness
alone.

As you read Scripture this year, hold all of this together: come with
confidence that its message is reachable, and come with prayerful
dependence, expecting the Spirit himself to do the opening.

## Prayer

Holy Spirit, open my eyes to see what your word is actually saying. I
don't want to rely on my own cleverness alone — give me understanding, and
surround me with others who can help me see what I might miss. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Do you tend to approach Bible reading more as an intellectual exercise, or
as a prayerful, Spirit-dependent one? What would it look like to hold both?
