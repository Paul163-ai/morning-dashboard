## Verse

"Now faith is the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things
not seen." — Hebrews 11:1

## Explanation

We turn now to the other half of conversion: faith. Theologians have
traditionally described saving faith as having three parts. First,
knowledge — you have to know something about Christ and the gospel to
believe it; faith is never a blind leap devoid of content. Second, assent —
you have to actually agree that what you know is true, that Jesus is who
he claimed to be and did what the gospel says he did.

But — and this is the crucial third part — knowledge and assent alone
aren't yet saving faith. Even demons "believe" in that sense (James 2:19),
and it does them no good at all. The third element is trust: personally
relying on Christ, resting your whole hope on him, the way you'd trust your
full weight to a chair rather than merely believing, intellectually, that
the chair could hold you. Faith is that leaning, that resting, not just
that agreeing.

Hebrews 11:1 captures this well — faith isn't primarily about certainty of
facts you can prove, but about confident reliance on promises not yet
fully seen. If you've ever wondered whether your faith is "real" because
you have doubts or unanswered questions, remember: saving faith was never
about having zero uncertainty. It's about where you place your trust when
uncertainty remains.

## Prayer

Lord, I know facts about you, and I believe they're true — but more than
that, I want to actually rest my whole weight on you. Where I lean on
myself instead, teach me to trust you fully. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Where in your life are you still leaning on your own strength rather than
truly resting your weight on Christ?
