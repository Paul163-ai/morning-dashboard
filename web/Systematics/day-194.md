## Verse

"I will give you a new heart, and a new spirit I will put within you. And
I will remove the heart of stone from your flesh and give you a heart of
flesh." — Ezekiel 36:26

## Explanation

Centuries before Jesus spoke to Nicodemus, God promised his people exactly
this through Ezekiel: not moral coaching, not a new set of rules, but a new
heart. A heart of stone can't be reasoned into loving God — it has to be
replaced. That's the picture regeneration gives us: God doesn't renovate
the old, resistant heart. He removes it and gives a new one, capable of
love and obedience for the first time.

This is why regeneration is described as God's work first, before faith
and repentance, not after. Titus 3:5 puts it starkly: God "saved us, not
because of works done by us in righteousness, but according to his own
mercy, by the washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit." A
stone heart cannot choose to become a heart of flesh. Only God can perform
that surgery — and once he does, faith and repentance follow naturally,
because a new heart wants what it once resisted.

If you've ever wondered why the gospel seemed like foolishness to you once
and now feels like the most obviously true thing in the world, this is
part of the answer. Something changed that you didn't do yourself. Give
thanks for the surgeon, not just the outcome.

## Prayer

Father, thank you for removing my heart of stone and giving me a heart
that can actually love and trust you. I didn't perform this surgery on
myself — you did. Keep softening my heart where it still resists you.
Amen.

## Reflection Question

Where do you still notice traces of a "stone heart" — resistance,
indifference, or hardness toward something God calls you to? Bring that to
him today.
