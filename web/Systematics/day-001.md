## Verse

"All Scripture is breathed out by God and profitable for teaching, for
reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness, that the man of
God may be complete, equipped for every good work." — 2 Timothy 3:16–17

## Explanation

Paul doesn't call Scripture "helpful" or "inspiring" — he calls it
*breathed out by God*. Every word of it traces back to the mouth of God
himself, spoken through human authors he prepared and carried along. That's
why we open a new year of daily readings here, on Day 1: not because
Scripture is one useful resource among many for knowing God, but because it
is the resource. Left to ourselves, we could never reason our way to who
God is, what he has done, or what he asks of us. We need him to speak — and
in Scripture, he has.

This is also why theology itself is possible. Systematic theology isn't
humans inventing ideas about God; it's the patient work of gathering what
God has already said across the whole Bible and letting it shape a coherent
understanding of him. Every doctrine we'll walk through this year — the
Trinity, sin, the cross, the church, the future — stands or falls on this
first conviction: God has spoken, and his words are trustworthy,
sufficient, and alive.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that you have not left me to guess who you are. Thank you
for breathing out your word so that I could know you, be corrected by you,
and be equipped to do good. As I begin this year, give me a hunger for your
word and a readiness to be shaped by it, not just informed by it. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Where in your life right now are you tempted to trust your own reasoning
about God over what he has actually said in Scripture?
