## Verse

"See to it that no one takes you captive by philosophy and empty deceit,
according to human tradition, according to the elemental spirits of the
world, and not according to Christ." — Colossians 2:8

## Explanation

Reason is a genuine gift from God, and Scripture never asks anyone to
switch it off — Christians are called to love God with their minds
(Matthew 22:37) and to test everything carefully (1 Thessalonians 5:21).
But Paul draws a clear line here: human philosophy and reasoning, however
sophisticated, can become a trap when it operates independently of Christ
and his revealed word, setting itself up as a rival authority rather than
a tool submitted to Scripture.

This matters because every generation faces plausible, intelligent
reasoning that quietly contradicts what God has said — whether ancient
philosophy or modern ideology. The temptation is always the same: "surely
this careful reasoning has uncovered something Scripture missed or got
wrong." Scripture's authority means resisting that temptation, however
compelling the reasoning sounds, and however unfashionable Scripture's
teaching might seem by comparison.

This doesn't mean abandoning careful thought — theology itself, this whole
year's project, is an exercise in reasoning carefully about what God has
revealed. It means reasoning under Scripture's authority, using your mind
to understand and apply God's word, rather than using it to overrule
God's word when it becomes inconvenient.

## Prayer

Father, give me a mind that reasons carefully under your word, not one
that quietly uses reasoning to escape what you've clearly said. Protect me
from being taken captive by plausible ideas that contradict Christ. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is there a teaching of Scripture you find intellectually uncomfortable,
where you've been tempted to let clever reasoning quietly override it?
