## Verse

"If I say, 'Surely the darkness shall cover me, and the light about me be
night,' even the darkness is not dark to you; the night is bright as the
day, for darkness is as light with you." — Psalm 139:11–12

## Explanation

David takes yesterday's truth and pushes it into the hardest places
imaginable — darkness itself, the place we most instinctively associate
with isolation, danger, and hiddenness. And even there, he says, God's
presence isn't dimmed or blocked. Darkness that conceals everything from
human eyes conceals nothing from God; it is, to him, as bright as day.

This has deep pastoral weight. Everyone eventually walks through seasons
that feel like darkness — grief, depression, crisis, seasons where God
feels distant or absent precisely because circumstances are so dark.
Omnipresence means that feeling, however real and painful, isn't
describing reality. God's actual presence doesn't withdraw when your
circumstances turn dark. He is exactly as fully present in your darkest
season as in your brightest one, even when you can't sense it.

This is also why no one, anywhere, is ever truly alone in their suffering
if they belong to God. Not in a hospital room at 3am, not in the depths
of depression, not in any circumstance that feels isolating and hidden
from everyone else. The God who is fully present everywhere is fully
present there too — his presence undimmed by whatever darkness surrounds
you.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that darkness is not dark to you. When I walk through
seasons that feel isolating or hidden, remind me that you are fully
present there, even when I can't sense you. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is there a dark season, past or present, where it would help to remember
God's presence was (or is) fully there, even if it didn't feel that way?
