## Verse

"For his invisible attributes, namely, his eternal power and divine
nature, have been clearly perceived, ever since the creation of the world,
in the things that have been made." — Romans 1:20

## Explanation

Alongside the inner sense of God we looked at yesterday, Scripture points
to an outer witness: creation itself. We touched on this back in our
study of general revelation, but it's worth returning to specifically as
evidence for God's existence. Paul's claim is bold — not that creation
offers a vague suggestion of something divine, but that God's power and
nature are "clearly perceived" through what has been made, with enough
force to leave every observer without excuse.

Across history, careful thinkers have pointed to different aspects of
this testimony: the sheer existence of anything at all, rather than
nothing, pointing to a necessary, uncaused cause behind all contingent
things; the intricate order and apparent design woven through the natural
world, from the structure of a cell to the fine-tuned constants that
allow the universe to exist at all; the reality of objective moral
values, which matter is not obviously equipped to produce on its own.
None of these arguments were needed to convince Paul creation testifies
to God — he treats it as plain and evident to anyone actually looking.

The question was never really whether evidence for God exists. It's
whether people are willing to actually look, and to let what they see lead
them where it points.

## Prayer

Creator God, thank you that everything you've made testifies to your
power and glory. Give me eyes to actually see it today, rather than
looking past it unnoticed. Amen.

What part of the created world speaks most clearly to you of God's power
