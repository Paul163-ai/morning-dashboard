## Verse

"He has put eternity into man's heart, yet so that he cannot find out what
God has done from the beginning to the end." — Ecclesiastes 3:11

## Explanation

We begin an entirely new section today — the doctrine of God himself —
starting with the most fundamental question there is: how do we know God
exists at all? Before we get to arguments or evidence, Scripture points to
something more basic: an inbuilt awareness, planted by God in every human
being, that there is more to reality than the material world in front of
us. The Preacher calls it "eternity in the heart" — a restless sense that
we belong to something beyond ourselves.

This sense doesn't arrive through study or argument. It's part of what it
means to be made in God's image, present in every culture, every era,
every person, however it's suppressed, distorted, or explained away.
It's why atheism has always had to argue against something, rather than
simply describing a natural, obvious human default. The instinct that God
exists is closer to home than a conclusion reached — it's a starting
awareness that has to be actively resisted to be denied.

This is worth remembering the next time you talk to someone who insists
they don't believe in God at all. Somewhere beneath the surface,
Scripture suggests, that sense of "eternity in the heart" is still there,
however buried.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you for placing an awareness of you deep within every human
heart, including mine, long before I understood who you were. Awaken that
sense in me freshly today. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Can you recall a moment, even before you were a believer, when you sensed
something beyond the material world — an "eternity in the heart" moment?
