## Verse

"Since we have been justified by faith, we have peace with God through our
Lord Jesus Christ." — Romans 5:1

## Explanation

Notice precisely what Paul says justifies: faith, not works, not
sacraments, not moral effort added alongside faith. This was the exact
issue at stake in the Reformation — whether justification comes by faith
alone, or by faith plus human contribution. Paul's letters, especially
Romans and Galatians, argue relentlessly for the former: "a person is not
justified by works of the law but through faith in Jesus Christ" (Galatians
2:16).

This doesn't mean faith is some meritorious work God rewards, as though
believing hard enough earns the verdict. Faith justifies only because of
what it lays hold of — Christ's own righteousness, credited to the
believer (a truth we'll spend more time on in a few days). Faith is the
instrument, not the ground, of justification. It's the empty hand
receiving, not the payment being offered.

The immediate fruit of this, Paul says, is peace with God — not a vague
feeling, but an objective reality: the war between a holy God and a
sinner has actually ended, once for all, through Christ. If you're still
striving to add something of your own to secure God's favour — extra
effort, religious performance, moral scorekeeping — you're reaching for
peace you already have. Justification by faith alone means the war is
already over.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that peace with you doesn't depend on what I add to
faith, but on Christ alone, received by faith alone. Free me from any
lingering effort to earn what you have already given. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is there anything you're quietly relying on alongside faith in Christ to
feel right with God — good deeds, religious habits, self-discipline?
