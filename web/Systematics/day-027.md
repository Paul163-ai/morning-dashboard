## Verse

"I warn everyone who hears the words of the prophecy of this book: if
anyone adds to them, God will add to him the plagues described in this
book." — Revelation 22:18

## Explanation

This solemn warning, closing the entire canon of Scripture, underlines
sufficiency from another angle: not just that Scripture provides enough,
but that adding to it — treating some further revelation, tradition, or
teaching as equally necessary and binding alongside it — is treated with
real seriousness. This isn't a minor stylistic preference. Throughout
history, from various movements claiming new prophetic authority to
traditions elevating human teaching to Scripture's level, the temptation
to add has recurred again and again.

This doesn't mean God has stopped speaking in every sense, or that
teaching, preaching, and wise counsel have no place — Scripture itself
commends good teaching abundantly. What it means is that nothing else can
be treated as equally authoritative, equally necessary, or binding on the
conscience the way Scripture is. Any teaching, tradition, or claimed
revelation must be tested against Scripture and can never be placed
alongside it as a co-equal source of authority.

This matters practically whenever you encounter a teacher, movement, or
tradition — however sincere or compelling — that asks you to receive
something as necessary for salvation or spiritual maturity beyond what
Scripture itself teaches. Sufficiency means you're free, and required, to
test it and hold the line.

## Prayer

Lord, keep me discerning wherever I encounter teaching that asks me to add
to your word as though it weren't enough. Let me test everything against
Scripture, and hold fast to what you've already sufficiently given. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Have you ever encountered teaching that quietly added extra requirements
to the gospel beyond what Scripture actually teaches? How did you (or how
should you) respond?
