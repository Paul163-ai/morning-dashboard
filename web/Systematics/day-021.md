## Verse

"The unfolding of your words gives light; it imparts understanding to the
simple." — Psalm 119:130

## Explanation

This week we look at a doctrine that offers real relief to anyone who's
ever felt intimidated opening the Bible: clarity, sometimes called
perspicuity. It's the teaching that Scripture's central message — who
God is, what's wrong with us, what Christ has done, how to be saved and
live for him — is clear enough to be genuinely understood by any ordinary
believer reading with sincerity, prayer, and the ordinary means available
to them, not just by scholars or specialists.

The Psalmist's language is deliberately humble: God's words give light
even "to the simple" — not the educated elite, not those with special
training, but ordinary people without particular sophistication. This was
one of the great convictions recovered at the Reformation: that the Bible
belongs, in its essential message, to every believer, not locked away as
the property of religious professionals who alone could interpret it
correctly.

This doesn't mean every single verse is equally easy, which we'll get to
tomorrow. But it does mean you don't need a theology degree to genuinely
understand what Scripture is telling you about the gospel, about God, and
about how to live. If you can read, and you come with humility and
prayer, God has made his central message reachable for you.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that your word wasn't hidden away for the educated few. I
come to it as I am, and ask you to give me understanding as I read,
however simple my starting point. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Have you ever avoided reading Scripture for yourself because you assumed
you'd need special training to understand it? What would change if you
believed its central message was genuinely reachable for you?
