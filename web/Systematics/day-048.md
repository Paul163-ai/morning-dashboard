## Verse

"Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one." — Deuteronomy 6:4

## Explanation

We return today to a verse we sat with during our week on the Trinity,
but from a different angle: God's unity. This attribute means two things
held together. First, numerically, there is only one God — not many gods,
not a hierarchy of divine beings, but a single, undivided divine being,
as we saw when we studied the Trinity: three persons, one being, not
three beings loosely cooperating.

Second, and just as important, God's unity means his character and
purposes are internally coherent — never divided against himself, never
torn between competing desires or contradictory impulses the way a
divided human heart often is. His love and his justice don't compete for
control of his actions; his mercy and his holiness aren't in tension
requiring one to lose so the other can win. Everything about God works
together in perfect harmony, because he is, in his very being, one and
undivided.

This matters for how you trust him. A God whose character was internally
inconsistent — loving one moment, harsh the next, for no coherent
reason — would be impossible to actually rely on. Because God is
genuinely one, undivided in being and character, everything he does,
however varied it appears across Scripture and across your own life,
flows from the same unified, trustworthy nature.

## Prayer

Lord, you are one — undivided in being, undivided in character. Thank you
that I can trust you aren't torn between conflicting impulses, but that
everything you do flows from one coherent, trustworthy nature. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Have you ever felt confused by seemingly different "sides" of God's
character — his love and his justice, for example? How does his unity
help hold those together?
