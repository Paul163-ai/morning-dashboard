## Verse

"The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand; repent and
believe in the gospel." — Mark 1:15

## Explanation

We close this week where conversion always shows up in Scripture: repent
and believe, held together as a single response, never presented as two
separate options or sequential stages you could complete independently.
You cannot genuinely believe in Christ as Saviour while still clinging to
the sin he came to save you from, and you cannot genuinely turn from sin
without somewhere to turn to. Repentance without faith is just remorse.
Faith without repentance isn't real trust in Christ at all — it's just
wanting his benefits while keeping your old life intact.

Think of it like turning around: repentance is the turning away from,
faith is the turning toward — but it's one movement, one pivot, not two
separate acts performed back to back. This is why conversion, however it
happens — dramatically or quietly, in a single moment or over unfolding
weeks — always involves both elements together, even if a person couldn't
have named them separately at the time.

If you're not sure whether you've genuinely turned to Christ, these two
questions can help: Have you actually turned away from trusting yourself,
your goodness, or anything else to make you right with God? And have you
actually turned toward Christ, resting your whole hope in him? If both are
true, however imperfectly lived out since, that's conversion — the
beginning of the story we'll trace onward from tomorrow into justification
itself.

## Prayer

Lord Jesus, I turn from trusting myself, my goodness, my own efforts to
make things right — and I turn to you, resting my whole hope in what you
have done. Keep me turning, daily, toward you. Amen.

## Reflection Question

If someone asked you to describe, in your own words, what you turned from
and what you turned to when you came to Christ, what would you say?
