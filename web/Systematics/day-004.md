## Verse

"That the man of God may be complete, equipped for every good work." —
2 Timothy 3:17

## Explanation

We close this first short section by looking at where all this is heading.
Scripture isn't given simply to satisfy curiosity about God or to win
theological arguments. Paul's stated purpose is intensely practical:
completeness, equipping, and good work. Right theology is meant to produce
a whole, useful life — not just a well-informed mind.

This should shape how you approach a whole year of doctrine ahead. It would
be possible to read every devotional this year, learn every doctrine
accurately, and still miss the point, if it never moved past information
into transformation. Every truth we study — about God's character, Christ's
work, the church's mission, our future hope — is aimed at making you
"complete," someone equipped to actually live and serve, not just someone
who can recite correct answers.

So as you begin, hold theology and life together from day one. Ask not
only "is this true?" but "how does this equip me?" A year spent gathering
theological knowledge without letting it shape love, obedience, and
service would be a year that missed exactly what Scripture itself says it
was given for.

## Prayer

Father, don't let this year of study become mere information for me.
Shape me through it — make me complete, and equip me for the good work you
have for me to do. Amen.

## Reflection Question

As you begin this year of theology, what's one "good work" you hope a
deeper knowledge of God will actually equip you for?
