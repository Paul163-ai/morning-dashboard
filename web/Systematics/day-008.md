## Verse

"In the past he let all nations go their own way. Yet he has not left
himself without testimony: He has shown kindness by giving you rain from
heaven and crops in their seasons." — Acts 14:16–17

## Explanation

A simple way to hold general and special revelation together: general
revelation reveals *that* God is — his existence, power, and glory,
available to everyone through creation. Special revelation reveals *who*
God is in saving detail — his character, his plan, and how sinners can be
reconciled to him, given specifically through Scripture and supremely
through Christ.

You need both, understood rightly, to have a full picture. General
revelation without special revelation leaves people with an accurate but
incomplete and ultimately condemning knowledge of God — enough to know he
exists, never enough to know how to be saved. Special revelation, read
without appreciating general revelation, can miss the fact that God has
always been actively present and testifying to himself even among people
who've never heard the gospel preached, as Paul makes clear speaking to
pagans in Lystra.

This should shape both humility and confidence in how you talk to
unbelievers about God. Humility, because they likely already sense
something true about God's existence and power, even if they suppress or
misname it. Confidence, because what they're missing — the specific,
saving content of the gospel — is exactly what you have to offer them.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you for revealing yourself both broadly, in creation, and
specifically, in your word and your Son. Help me engage people who don't
yet know you with both humility about what they already sense, and
confidence in what I can tell them. Amen.

## Reflection Question

In a conversation with someone who doesn't believe, how might
acknowledging what they may already sense about God (through general
revelation) open the door to sharing what they're missing?
