## Verse

"In him you also, when you heard the word of truth, the gospel of your
salvation, and believed in him, were sealed with the promised Holy Spirit,
who is the guarantee of our inheritance." — Ephesians 1:13–14

## Explanation

We finish this section of the year the way it should end: not with an idea
about the Spirit, but with the Spirit's own presence in you. Paul says the
moment you believed, God sealed you. A seal marks ownership and guarantees
authenticity — a letter sealed with wax couldn't be tampered with or doubted.
That's what the Spirit's presence in a believer is: God's own mark, saying
"this one is mine, and I will finish what I started in them."

Paul goes further and calls the Spirit a "guarantee" — literally a down
payment, the first installment of something bigger still to come. The Spirit
you have now, who convicts, comforts, teaches, and prays for you, is not the
whole of what God has for you. He's the deposit. The rest — a resurrection
body, a renewed creation, unbroken fellowship with God — is coming, and the
Spirit's presence in you now is God's own promise that it will.

This is a fitting hinge point. Everything we've studied about Christ's
person and work means nothing to us personally until the Spirit applies it.
From tomorrow, we turn to exactly that: how the salvation Christ achieved
becomes yours — grace, calling, regeneration, faith, justification. But
before we trace the steps, it's worth pausing on this: if you believe, you
are already sealed. Nothing about what follows is in doubt.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that your Spirit in me is not a maybe but a guarantee.
Thank you that you have marked me as your own and promised to finish what
you started. Help me live today in the confidence of that seal, not in
anxious uncertainty about whether I belong to you. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Do you tend to treat your salvation as settled and secure, or as something
still uncertain? How does calling the Spirit a "guarantee" speak to that?
