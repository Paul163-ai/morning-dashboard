## Verse

"For although they knew God, they did not honor him as God or give thanks
to him, but they became futile in their thinking, and their foolish
hearts were darkened." — Romans 1:21

## Explanation

We close January with the uncomfortable half of Paul's argument. It's not
that unbelievers lack access to evidence of God's existence — Paul has
just said the opposite, that his power and nature are clearly perceived.
The problem, Scripture says, is suppression: people "knew God," at some
level, and actively resisted honouring him for it, and that resistance
had consequences — futile thinking, darkened hearts, a downward spiral
into confusion and idolatry (vv.22–23).

This reframes how you might think about genuine atheism or agnosticism.
It's rarely, Scripture suggests, a simple, neutral absence of evidence
calmly considered and found wanting. It's more often an active,
often costly suppression of what's actually being perceived, because
honouring God as God would require submission, gratitude, and a change of
life that the human heart, left to itself, resists.

This should shape both compassion and honesty in how you engage with
someone who says they don't believe. Compassion, because this suppression
is the universal human condition apart from grace — you were there too,
before God opened your eyes. And honesty, because the conversation isn't
really about a shortage of evidence. It's about a heart, like every
heart apart from grace, resisting what it already senses is true.
Tomorrow, we turn to what it actually means to know this God, not just to
know that he exists.

## Prayer

Father, thank you for opening my own eyes when I, too, was suppressing
what I sensed about you. Give me compassion for those still resisting, and
help me speak the truth to them with both gentleness and clarity. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Before you believed, or in seasons of doubt since, have you recognised in
yourself the pattern of suppressing rather than simply lacking evidence?
