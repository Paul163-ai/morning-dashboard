## Verse

"Father, glorify your Son that the Son may glorify you... Father, I desire
that they also, whom you have given me, may be with me where I am, to see
my glory that you have given me because you loved me before the
foundation of the world." — John 17:1, 24

## Explanation

Here's why the Trinity isn't just a doctrine to affirm carefully and then
set aside as abstract mystery: it means God has never been alone, and
love and relationship are not things God merely does toward creation —
they're part of who he eternally is, within himself, before anything else
existed. The Father loved the Son "before the foundation of the world."
Glory, love, and mutual delight were already flowing between the persons
of the Trinity long before creation gave God anyone else to love.

This matters enormously for what it means that God created and loves you.
A solitary, single-person god would need creation in order to have
anyone to love, making his love for you something he needed in order to
be complete. The Trinitarian God needed nothing of the sort — he was
already, eternally, a communion of perfect love within himself. His love
for you, then, is pure overflow, not need. You were never created to fill
a gap in God's life. You were invited into a love and joy that already,
eternally, existed.

This is also the deep root of why relationship matters so much to human
beings, made in this God's image: we were made by, and for, a God whose
very nature is loving relationship between persons.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that your love for me isn't need, but overflow —
love that already existed eternally between you, the Son, and the Spirit,
before the world began. Let me rest in being invited into that love, not
required to complete it. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Does it change anything for you to know God's love toward you flows from
overflowing fullness, not neediness?
