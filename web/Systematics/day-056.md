## Verse

"Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, coming down from
the Father of lights, with whom there is no variation or shadow due to
change." — James 1:17

## Explanation

If God is the source and standard of goodness, as we saw yesterday,
James draws the natural conclusion: every genuinely good thing you've
ever received, however ordinary or unnoticed, traces back to him. Not
just the obviously spiritual blessings, but ordinary daily gifts —
friendship, a good meal, a moment of beauty, unexpected kindness. James
calls this the Father "of lights," probably evoking the sun, moon, and
stars he created — the giver of light itself, and, by extension, every
good thing that flows from it.

Notice too the reassurance James adds: "no variation or shadow due to
change." This is unchangeableness, from earlier this month, applied
directly to God's generosity: he isn't a giver whose kindness ebbs and
flows unpredictably, generous one season and withholding the next. His
goodness toward you is as constant as his character, because it flows
directly from his character.

This should shape ordinary gratitude into something richer than a vague
sense that "things are going well." Every specific good thing in your
life today — however small — is a specific gift from a specific, good,
unchanging Father, worth naming and thanking him for individually, not
just acknowledging in general.

## Prayer

Father of lights, thank you for every good gift, ordinary and
extraordinary, that has come from your hand. Help me notice and name
specific gifts today, rather than letting your generosity go unnoticed.
Amen.

## Reflection Question

Name three specific, ordinary good things in your life right now. Have
you actually thanked God for each of them by name?
