## Verse

"Until heaven and earth pass away, not an iota, not a dot, will pass from
the Law until all is accomplished." — Matthew 5:18

## Explanation

If you want to know how seriously to take Scripture's claim to be God's
own word, there's no better place to look than how Jesus himself treated
it. He didn't hold the Old Testament loosely, or treat it as a flawed
human document needing correction from his superior insight. He affirmed
even its smallest details — "not an iota, not a dot" — as certain to be
fulfilled, precisely because it carried God's own authority.

Throughout the Gospels, Jesus settles arguments by appealing to what
"is written" (Matthew 4:4, 7, 10), treats Old Testament events and
figures — Adam and Eve, Noah, Jonah — as straightforward history, not
symbolic legend, and submits his own life and death to what the Scriptures
said must happen (Luke 24:25–27, 44–46). If Jesus is who Christians
believe him to be — God incarnate — then his view of Scripture isn't
merely one opinion among others to weigh. It's the verdict of the one who
inspired it in the first place.

This closes the loop on this week's theme: Scripture's claim to be God's
own word isn't only the prophets' and apostles' testimony about
themselves. It's also, decisively, the Son of God's own testimony about
the book he came to fulfil.

## Prayer

Lord Jesus, thank you for the example you set in trusting and submitting
to Scripture completely. Let your view of the Bible shape mine, above any
other voice or opinion. Amen.

## Reflection Question

If Jesus' own confidence in Scripture is the standard, where might your
own confidence in it need to grow?
