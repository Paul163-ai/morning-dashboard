## Verse

"Nor is he served by human hands, as though he needed anything, since he
himself gives to all mankind life and breath and everything." —
Acts 17:25

## Explanation

Having spent a week on the Trinity, we now turn to God's attributes —
the specific qualities of his character Scripture reveals. We begin with
what theologians call independence, or aseity: God exists entirely from
himself, dependent on nothing and no one, needing nothing outside himself
to be complete, happy, or fully God. Paul states this plainly to a crowd
in Athens accustomed to gods who needed feeding, housing, and appeasing:
the true God needs nothing from human hands, because he is the giver of
everything, never the receiver of anything he actually lacks.

This connects directly to what we saw last week about the Trinity: God
was never lonely or incomplete before creation, because Father, Son, and
Spirit already possessed perfect love and fellowship within the divine
being itself. Creation, then, wasn't God solving a problem or filling a
need. It was pure, overflowing generosity — bringing into existence
creatures he didn't require, purely because he chose to.

This should reshape how you think about your own relationship with God.
He doesn't need your worship, service, or obedience to become more
complete or more glorious than he already, eternally is. Everything you
offer him is response to grace already given, never a contribution he was
lacking.

## Prayer

Father, thank you that you need nothing from me — that my worship and
service are gifts I offer in response to your grace, not contributions
you require. Let that free me from performing for you and move me toward
genuine, grateful worship. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Have you ever unconsciously approached God as though your service or
performance were something he needed? How does his independence correct
that?
