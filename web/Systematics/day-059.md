## Verse

"But God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners,
Christ died for us." — Romans 5:8

## Explanation

We close this second month of the year at the clearest, most concrete
demonstration of everything we've studied about God's love: the cross.
Notice Paul's timing carefully — not "after we cleaned ourselves up,"
not "once we showed some sign of deserving it," but while we were still
sinners, actively opposed to God, Christ died. God's love wasn't a
response to something lovable he found in us. It was love that created
its own reason, poured out on people who had given him every reason to
withhold it.

This is the decisive proof that God's love, unlike so much human love, is
never conditional on merit. It doesn't wait for you to become good enough
to deserve it, and it isn't withdrawn when you fail to live up to it
afterward. The same love that reached you "while you were still a
sinner" doesn't suddenly become conditional now that you've believed —
it's the same unearned, freely given love, continuing to hold you, on
your best days and your worst.

We've spent this whole month working through who God is: incomprehensible
yet knowable, three persons in perfect unity, independent yet freely
loving, unchanging, eternal, everywhere present, one, all-knowing, wise,
truthful, good, and loving beyond anything human love can fully picture.
Next month, we turn to the rest of God's attributes — mercy, holiness,
righteousness, and more — continuing to trace the full character of the
God this whole year is really about knowing.

## Prayer

Father, thank you for proving your love at the cross — not waiting for me
to deserve it, but giving everything while I was still a sinner. Let that
same unearned love hold me today, whatever kind of day it has been. Amen.
