## Verse

"His divine power has granted to us all things that pertain to life and
godliness, through the knowledge of him who called us to his own glory and
excellence." — 2 Peter 1:3

## Explanation

Sufficiency doesn't stop at salvation — it extends to how you're meant to
actually live afterward. Peter is emphatic: "all things that pertain to
life and godliness" have already been granted, through what God has made
known. This rules out the idea that Scripture gets a Christian started,
but that real spiritual maturity or the ability to actually live
faithfully requires some further, separate resource — special techniques,
newer insight, or a level of experience Scripture itself doesn't provide.

This doesn't mean Scripture explicitly addresses every specific modern
circumstance by name — it doesn't tell you which job to take or how to
navigate a particular difficult conversation with precise instructions.
But it means Scripture provides everything necessary, in principle and
in wisdom, to work out godly living in any circumstance: the character of
God to trust, the pattern of Christ to follow, the wisdom to apply, and
the promises to rest in. Applying it well takes wisdom and often
counsel, but the raw material was never missing.

If you've ever felt like real spiritual growth requires some technique or
experience beyond ordinary engagement with Scripture, prayer, and the
church, sufficiency corrects that instinct. What you need for life and
godliness, you already have.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that you've given me everything I need for a godly life,
not just a start. Keep me from chasing after what you've already provided,
and give me wisdom to apply it well. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Is there an area of your spiritual life where you've been searching for a
"missing ingredient" rather than trusting what Scripture has already given
you?
