## Verse

"No prophecy was ever produced by the will of man, but men spoke from God
as they were carried along by the Holy Spirit." — 2 Peter 1:21

## Explanation

It's worth being precise about what kind of book the Bible claims to be.
It isn't a collection of humanity's best guesses about God, refined over
centuries the way philosophy or religion generally develops. Peter says the
opposite direction was true: the prophets weren't the origin of what they
wrote — they were carried along, moved by the Holy Spirit, so that what
emerged was, in origin, from God.

This doesn't erase the human authors. Isaiah still sounds like Isaiah, Paul
still argues like a trained rabbi, and each writer's personality, style,
and circumstances are visible throughout Scripture. God didn't dictate
mechanically, bypassing the humanity of his authors. But the origin, the
ultimate authorship, the reason any of it can be trusted, is not human
insight — it's the Spirit carrying real people along so that their genuine
words were also, fully, God's own words.

This is why Scripture can be read as an actual conversation, not a museum
piece. When you open the Bible, you're not examining ancient human opinion
about God from a safe historical distance. You're hearing God speak — as
much today as when the ink first dried.

## Prayer

Holy Spirit, thank you for carrying along the writers of Scripture so that
their words became your words. Help me read the Bible not as ancient
opinion but as you speaking to me today. Amen.

## Reflection Question

Do you tend to read Scripture as a historical document about God, or as
God actively speaking to you now? What might change if it were the
latter?
