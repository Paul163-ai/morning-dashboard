## Verse

"How then will they call on him in whom they have not believed? And how are
they to believe in him of whom they have never heard? And how are they to
hear without someone preaching?" — Romans 10:14

## Explanation

We move now from election — God's hidden, eternal choice — to something
altogether public: the gospel call. This is the invitation of the gospel
as it's actually preached and offered to everyone who hears it, without
exception or condition. Theologians distinguish this "general call" from
the "effective call" we'll look at next week; the general call goes out
broadly and can be resisted, while the effective call is God's inward work
that actually brings someone to faith.

Paul's logic here matters: salvation doesn't happen by God's secret decree
alone, floating free of any human means. It happens through hearing —
someone must preach, someone must go, someone must speak the actual words
of the gospel for it to be believed. Election never bypasses this ordinary
chain. God has bound together his sovereign purpose and the plain,
unglamorous work of gospel proclamation.

This is a good corrective to any temptation to treat mission as optional
or theology as merely academic. Somewhere, right now, people have not heard
because no one has told them. The doctrine of the gospel call isn't
abstract — it's a summons to be part of the "how are they to hear" chain
Paul describes, whether that's across the world or across your street.

## Prayer

Lord, thank you that the gospel is not hidden but offered freely and
openly to all. Make me willing to be part of the chain of people carrying
that call to those who haven't heard — whether far away or close to home.
Amen.

## Reflection Question

Who around you has genuinely never heard the gospel clearly explained?
What would it look like to be the one who tells them?
